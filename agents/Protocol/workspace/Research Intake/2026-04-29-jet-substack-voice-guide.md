# Jet Substack Voice Guide — for rewriting The Protocol Issue 001

Source corpus: `2026-04-29-jet-substack-corpus.md`  
Target draft: `Drafts/2026-04-29-the-protocol-issue-001-ai-operating-memo.md`  
Use case: rewrite The Protocol for Thai business owners/founders/C-suite in Jet’s pragmatic, peer-to-peer, quietly confident voice.

## 1. Core voice

Jet writes like an operator talking to another operator after paying the tuition himself.

The voice is:
- **Direct, practical, and impatient with theory.** He repeatedly frames ideas as “not theory,” “not fluffy,” “not another tool,” but a decision/action/process.
- **Peer-to-peer, not professor-to-student.** He writes as someone in the arena: “I spent…”, “I’ve seen…”, “I learned this the hard way.”
- **Quietly but firmly confident.** The certainty comes from scars, testing, spend, lived business experience, and patterns from training leaders — not from sounding academic.
- **Business-first.** AI is never just technology. It is cost, speed, hiring, standards, sales, service, customer retention, operating rhythm.
- **Action-biased.** Each piece pushes the reader toward one concrete move this week/today.

For The Protocol, this means: write less like a polished strategy memo and more like Jet saying, “I’ve watched Thai businesses get this wrong. Here’s the better move. Do this on Monday.”

## 2. Recurring structure

### A. Strong hook with stakes

Jet usually opens with one of these patterns:

1. **Specific personal investment/scar**  
   Example pattern: “I spent $30,000 testing AI tools so you don’t have to.” / “This one mistake cost us $30K.”

2. **Contrarian diagnosis**  
   Example pattern: “The real AI divide isn’t technical. It’s operational.” / “Traditional leadership is dead.”

3. **Specific scene**  
   Example pattern: “Last week, I sat down with…” / “I was standing at the edge…” / “Picture this…”

4. **Reader-facing warning**  
   Example pattern: “You’re probably making the same mistake…” / “If you’re still leading like it’s 2019…”

For Issue 001, strongest hook direction:

> เจ้าของธุรกิจไทยส่วนใหญ่ไม่ได้แพ้ AI เพราะไม่มี tool  
> เขาแพ้เพราะยังไม่มี operating system ให้ AI เข้าไปทำงานจริง

or

> ผมเห็นเจ้าของธุรกิจจำนวนมากเริ่ม AI ผิดที่  
> เขาเริ่มจาก “tool อะไรดี?”  
> ทั้งที่คำถามนี้มักพาบริษัทไปเสียเวลา เสียเงิน และได้แค่ demo ที่ดูว้าวแต่ไม่เปลี่ยนวิธีทำงานจริง

### B. Hook → diagnosis → framework → action

Most successful posts follow this backbone:

1. **Hook/stakes** — why this matters now.
2. **The mistake or misconception** — what most people get wrong.
3. **The reframe** — the better operating belief.
4. **Framework/list** — 3 steps, 4 pillars, 5-step blueprint, 9 tools, etc.
5. **Specific action plan** — today/this week/next 90 days.
6. **Question CTA** — “Reply and tell me…”
7. **Soft offer/PS** — DM keyword, workshop, community, coaching.

Apply to Issue 001:

1. Hook: AI is not an IT project; it is an operations redesign.
2. Mistake: buying tools / asking team to “try ChatGPT.”
3. Reframe: start from recurring workflows close to money/customers.
4. Framework: 3-part AI Operating Memo: Task → AI step → Human approval/measurement.
5. Action: 60–90 minute Monday workflow audit.
6. CTA: reply with one recurring process; Jet may break it down.
7. Soft offer: Limitless Club/workshop helps operators build this systematically.

## 3. Sentence style

### What Jet does often

- **Short paragraphs.** Often 1–3 sentences. Many one-line paragraphs.
- **Punchy fragments.** “Big mistake.” “Dead wrong.” “No middle ground.” “That is it.”
- **Question-answer rhythm.** “Why? Because…” / “The mistake? Getting soft.” / “The real power? Integrations.”
- **Contrast pairs.** “Not X. Y.” / “Tools don’t win. Systems do.”
- **Declarative certainty.** “The goal isn’t…” “The question isn’t…” “This is where…”
- **Plain language with business terms.** Uses English business/AI terms naturally inside Thai/English: workflow, stack, operator, AI-first, no-brainer, prompt, SOP, conversion.

### For Thai default language

Keep Thai conversational and business-owner friendly. Jet’s Thai in corpus freely mixes English terms where Thai business readers would use them:
- tool, workflow, stack, lead, sales, prompt, human approval, KPI, conversion, automation, operator, AI-first

Avoid over-translating technical terms into formal Thai. “workflow” is better than “กระบวนการไหลของงาน.”

### Rewrite principle

Current draft is clear but a bit memo-like. Inject more Jet rhythm:

Instead of:
> คำถามนี้ไม่ผิดครับ แต่ถ้าเริ่มตรงนี้ บริษัทจะได้แค่คนในทีมบางคนที่เก่งขึ้นนิดหน่อย

More Jet:

> แต่มันเล็กเกินไป

> แต่บริษัทไม่ได้เร็วขึ้นจริง

## 4. Rhetorical moves to emulate

### A. “Here’s what most people get wrong”

This is one of Jet’s most repeated moves. It creates authority without sounding abstract.

Use in Issue 001:
> สิ่งที่เจ้าของธุรกิจส่วนใหญ่เข้าใจผิดคือ AI ไม่ได้ล้มเหลวเพราะทีมไม่เก่ง technology  
> มันล้มเหลวเพราะไม่มีใครออกแบบงานใหม่ให้ AI เข้าไปเป็นส่วนหนึ่งของระบบ

### B. “Not X. Y.”

Examples from corpus include “Not as a helper. As the operator.” and repeated contrasts like “Not your business plan. Your physical health.”

Use:
> ไม่ใช่เพิ่ม tool เข้าไปในงานเดิม  
> แต่ถามใหม่ว่า ถ้า AI เป็น default executor งานนี้ควรถูกออกแบบใหม่อย่างไร

### C. Specific numbers create credibility

Jet uses money, time, counts, dates, and operational metrics constantly: $30K, 100 hours, 18 tools, 2 hours, 90 days, 20 leads, 80%.

Use numbers in Issue 001:
- 1 workflow
- 60–90 minutes
- 20 leads / 10 customer questions / 5 meetings
- 3 measurements
- 80% of repetitive work

### D. “The question isn’t… The question is…”

Use for AI positioning:
> คำถามไม่ใช่ “บริษัทเราควรใช้ AI ไหม?”  
> คำถามคือ “งานไหนในบริษัทที่เรายังให้คนทำซ้ำ ทั้งที่ AI ทำได้ 80% แล้ว?”

### E. Make business stakes concrete

Jet rarely leaves ideas at trend level. He converts them to payroll, response time, customer experience, hiring, standards, sales.

For Protocol, connect AI to:
- response time to leads
- fewer dropped action items
- consistent customer answers
- less dependency on one “hero” employee
- faster decision-making
- better service standard

### F. “Tiny but real” action

Jet’s action steps are not vague inspiration. They are operational:
- block two hours
- list recurring tasks
- rank by team hours
- choose first deployment target
- run it with 20 leads

Issue 001 should end with a Monday-morning task:
> เปิด blank doc  
> เขียนงานซ้ำ 10 งาน  
> เลือก 1 งานที่ใกล้เงินที่สุด  
> เขียน input / AI step / human approval / measurement  
> ลองใช้ 20 ครั้งก่อนซื้อ tool ใหม่

## 6. Self-disclosure pattern

Jet discloses personal mistakes and experience, but usually in service of the lesson. It is not confessional for its own sake.

Patterns:
- “I spent money/time testing this so you don’t have to.”
- “I learned this the hard way.”
- “I’ve seen this across business owners I trained/coached.”
- “I made this mistake in my own company.”
- “Looking back, I take responsibility.”

For Issue 001, add light self-disclosure if true/appropriate:
> ผมเห็น pattern นี้ซ้ำ ๆ เวลาคุยกับเจ้าของธุรกิจและทีม leadership  
> คนที่ได้ผลเร็วไม่ใช่คนที่มี tool เยอะที่สุด  
> แต่เป็นคนที่เลือก process เดียว แล้วออกแบบใหม่ตั้งแต่ input ถึง output

Avoid inventing specific claims unless Jet confirms them. The corpus supports general claims like training 1000s leaders, testing many AI tools, working with business owners, and building businesses.

- “DM me ‘COACH’ on Instagram…”
- “Share this with one entrepreneur…”
- “What’s your next move?”

For Protocol Issue 001:

Primary CTA:

## 8. Phrases to emulate

Use sparingly; adapt into Thai where natural.

- “Here’s the uncomfortable truth…” → “นี่คือความจริงที่หลายคนไม่อยากพูด…”
- “Most people get this wrong.” → “คนส่วนใหญ่พลาดตรงนี้…”
- “The question isn’t X. The question is Y.” → “คำถามไม่ใช่… คำถามคือ…”
- “Not as a helper. As the operator.” → “ไม่ใช่ผู้ช่วย แต่เป็น operator ของงานนั้น”
- “No fluff.” → “ไม่มีน้ำ” / “ไม่ใช่ theory”
- “Move the needle.” → “เปลี่ยนผลลัพธ์จริง”
- “Your move.” → “ตาคุณแล้ว” or leave as “Your move.” if brand voice allows
- “Do this this week.” → “ทำสิ่งนี้ภายในสัปดาห์นี้”
- “The window for exploration has closed.” → “ช่วงลองเล่นเฉย ๆ กำลังจบแล้ว”
- “AI as assistant → AI as operator.” → “จาก AI ที่ช่วยตอบ → AI ที่ช่วยทำงานเป็นขั้นตอน”
- “Systems over superheroes.” → “ระบบชนะ hero” / “อย่าให้บริษัทพึ่ง hero ตลอดเวลา”

## 9. Phrases/patterns to avoid for this project

Because The Protocol is for Thai founders/C-suite and should be pragmatic/quietly confident, avoid overusing the more extreme motivational side of the corpus:

- Too much apocalypse language: “your business is dead,” “get left behind,” “die,” unless softened.
- Too many bro-motivation lines: “crushing it,” “built for greatness,” “dreams go to die.”
- Overly American hype: “empire,” “world domination,” “10x everything,” unless the issue intentionally leans motivational.
- Too many emojis. The briefing format can use section markers, but the first issue should feel premium and operational.
- Long inspirational autobiography. Use self-disclosure only to create credibility for the operating lesson.
- Generic trend commentary without an action.
- Academic/consulting language like “digital transformation initiative,” “organizational capability building,” “strategic enablement.” Jet’s language is simpler and sharper.

## 10. Practical rewrite notes for current draft

The existing draft already has the right thesis and Thai business context. To make it sound more like Jet:

1. **Sharpen the opening.** Start with a stronger mistake/stakes hook before explaining The Protocol.
2. **Add one personal credibility paragraph.** Example: “ผมเห็น pattern นี้ซ้ำ ๆ…” Keep it grounded, not boastful.
3. **Break paragraphs shorter.** Many current paragraphs are memo-style. Turn key points into one-line punches.
4. **Use more contrast.** “Tool vs workflow,” “assistant vs operator,” “demo vs deployment,” “hero vs system.”
5. **Convert the action into a named protocol.** Jet often names frameworks. Example: “The Monday AI Workflow Audit” / “AI Workflow หน้าเดียว.”
6. **Make the CTA more Jet-like.** “Reply with one recurring task. I read every response.”
7. **Reduce soft abstract phrasing.** Replace “นี่คือภาษาของ operator” with a sharper line like “นี่คือวิธีคิดของ operator ไม่ใช่คนขาย software.”
