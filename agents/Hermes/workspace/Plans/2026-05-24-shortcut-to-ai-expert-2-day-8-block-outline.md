# Shortcut to AI Expert — 2-Day / 8-Block Comprehensive Outline

**Date drafted:** 2026-05-24  
**Format:** 2 full days, 4 blocks per day = 8 learning blocks  
**Audience:** Thai business owners / creators / operators who want to become practical AI operators, not just tool users  
**Primary outcome:** Every participant leaves with a working personal/business AI Team OS, a portfolio of useful workflows, and a peer community map for continued collaboration.

---

## Core Promise

By the end of two days, participants should be able to:

1. Think like an AI operator: identify leverage, choose the right tool/model, design workflows, and evaluate outputs.
2. Build a practical AI workspace for their own business/life.
3. Create reusable prompts, agents, automations, and content/research systems.
4. Turn messy personal/business knowledge into a searchable operating system.
5. Present their AI system to peers and get matched with collaborators.
6. Leave with a 30-day implementation roadmap.

---

## Workshop Design Principles

- **Maximum value:** Every block produces a tangible artifact.
- **Community-first:** Every participant shares, pairs, teaches, and gets matched by goals.
- **Beginner-safe, advanced-useful:** Start with simple wins, then layer strategy and systems.
- **Thai business reality:** Use examples from sales, content, customer service, operations, teaching, and local business workflows.
- **From tool usage to AI Team OS:** The workshop is not “100 AI tools.” It is how to build an AI-assisted way of working.
- **Show, build, reflect, share:** Each block cycles through demo → hands-on build → peer feedback → improvement.

# Day 1 Theme: From AI User to AI Operator

Day 1 takes participants from scattered AI usage to a clear personal/business AI operating system.

## Block 1 — Morning 1: Identity Shift + AI Leverage Map

**Theme:** “Stop using AI like a search box. Start using it like a team.”

### Outcomes

Participants leave with:

- Their AI Operator Scorecard.
- A personal/business leverage map.
- A clear workshop goal.
- A peer pod of 3–5 people.

### Teaching Points

- The difference between:
  - AI user
  - Prompt user
  - AI operator
  - AI team builder
- Why most people get weak AI results:
  - vague goals
  - weak context
  - no reusable workflow
  - no evaluation standard
  - no memory/source of truth
- The AI leverage stack:
  1. Tasks
  2. Workflows
  3. Knowledge base
  4. Agents
  5. Automation
  6. Business model redesign

### Activities

1. **Opening energy:** “One AI win / one AI frustration” pair share.
2. **AI Operator Self-Assessment:** rate current ability across research, writing, image/video, automation, data, and decision support.
3. **Leverage Map Exercise:** list all weekly tasks, then classify:
   - automate
   - augment
   - delegate to AI
   - keep human
4. **Community Pod Formation:** match by business type, goal, and skill level.
5. **Pod Contract:** each pod agrees how they will help each other during two days.

### Artifact

- `My AI Leverage Map`
- `My 2-Day AI Transformation Goal`
- Pod roster + shared goal board

### Community Mechanic

Create “AI Buddy Pods” that stay together for both days. Each pod has:

- Builder: most technical
- Storyteller: best at explaining
- Operator: best at business/process
- Encourager: keeps group moving

## Block 2 — Morning 2: Prompting as Management Skill

**Theme:** “Prompting is not magic words. It is delegation.”

### Outcomes

Participants leave with:

- A reusable prompt framework.
- 3 high-value prompts for their own business.
- A method for improving bad AI output.

### Teaching Points

- Prompting as delegation:
  - Role
  - Context
  - Objective
  - Constraints
  - Examples
  - Output format
  - Evaluation criteria
- How to give AI memory/context without overwhelming it.
- How to critique and iterate.
- How to ask AI to ask better questions.
- How to build prompt templates, not one-off prompts.

### Activities

1. **Bad Prompt → Great Prompt Demo:** show weak vs strong result.
2. **Prompt Deconstruction:** participants identify missing parts.
3. **Build 3 Core Prompts:**
   - Revenue/sales prompt
   - Content/marketing prompt
   - Operations/admin prompt
4. **Peer Prompt Swap:** each person gives their prompt to another person to test.
5. **Prompt Clinic:** improve prompts live using evaluation criteria.

### Artifact

- `My Prompt Management Framework`
- 3 reusable prompt templates
- Before/after prompt improvement example

### Community Mechanic

Prompt marketplace wall: every participant posts their best prompt into a shared library. Others can copy, adapt, and vote.

## Block 3 — Afternoon 1: Personal / Business Knowledge Base

**Theme:** “AI becomes powerful when it knows your world.”

### Outcomes

Participants leave with:

- A clean structure for their AI knowledge base.
- A first version of their business context document.
- A method for converting messy files/transcripts/notes into usable AI memory.

### Teaching Points

- Why AI needs source of truth.
- What belongs in a knowledge base:
  - customer avatar
  - offers
  - FAQs
  - brand voice
  - products/services
  - past content
  - SOPs
  - decisions
  - meeting notes
- Difference between:
  - raw notes
  - structured knowledge
  - reusable prompt context
  - agent memory
- How to avoid knowledge chaos.

### Activities

1. **Business Brain Dump:** participants write everything AI should know about their business.
2. **Context Compression:** turn the brain dump into a one-page business profile.
3. **Source Sorting:** classify sample files into:
   - keep
   - summarize
   - turn into SOP
   - ignore
4. **AI Context Test:** feed the profile into AI and ask it to advise on a real business question.
5. **Pod Review:** peers check whether the AI profile feels accurate.

### Artifact

- `Business AI Context Profile v1`
- `Knowledge Base Folder Structure`
- `Top 10 Sources AI Should Know`

### Community Mechanic

“Know Me Better” exercise: each participant gives their profile to a pod member, who asks AI to describe the business. If the owner says “that sounds like me,” the profile passes.

## Block 4 — Afternoon 2: Content + Offer Creation Engine

**Theme:** “Turn expertise into content, campaigns, and offers faster.”

### Outcomes

Participants leave with:

- A 30-day content idea bank.
- One polished content piece or campaign draft.
- One offer/message improvement using AI.

### Teaching Points

- Content engine structure:
  1. Source material
  2. Insight extraction
  3. Angle generation
  4. Format adaptation
  5. Human editing
  6. Distribution
- How to make content sound human, not AI-generated.
- How to use AI for Thai/English content carefully.
- How to repurpose one idea into many formats.
- How to connect content to offers and revenue.

### Activities

1. **Content Source Mining:** use participants’ own notes, posts, videos, or product pages.
2. **Angle Storm:** generate 20 hooks/angles.
3. **Choose One Campaign:** pick an audience + pain point + transformation.
4. **Create Assets:** write one post, short video script, carousel outline, or email.
5. **Humanizer Pass:** edit for voice, specificity, and credibility.
6. **Peer Feedback:** pod members rate clarity, trust, and conversion.

### Artifact

- `30-Day Content Idea Bank`
- One polished content/campaign draft
- `Offer Message Before/After`

### Community Mechanic

Gallery walk: participants display one output. Others leave sticky-note feedback: “clear,” “useful,” “I would buy/click because…,” “confusing because…”

# Day 2 Theme: From AI Workflows to AI Team OS

Day 2 turns skills into systems: agents, automations, evaluation, and implementation plans.

## Block 5 — Morning 1: AI Agents and Role-Based Workflows

**Theme:** “Design your AI team before you automate anything.”

### Outcomes

Participants leave with:

- A map of their ideal AI team.
- 3 role-based AI agent briefs.
- A tested workflow where one AI role produces useful output.

### Teaching Points

- What an agent really is:
  - role
  - context
  - tools
  - process
  - memory
  - output standard
- Examples:
  - Sales assistant
  - Research assistant
  - Content strategist
  - Customer support assistant
  - Finance/admin assistant
  - Learning coach
- When not to use agents.
- Human approval checkpoints.

### Activities

1. **AI Org Chart:** participants design their AI team for their business/life.
2. **Role Brief Template:** define one agent:
   - mission
   - inputs
   - outputs
   - tone
   - tools
   - boundaries
   - success criteria
3. **Agent Simulation:** use a chat model as one role and run a real task.
4. **Handoff Chain:** agent 1 creates output; agent 2 reviews/improves it.
5. **Pod Debugging:** peers identify missing context or unsafe assumptions.

### Artifact

- `My AI Team Org Chart`
- 3 AI role briefs
- One working agent-style workflow

### Community Mechanic

“Hire My Agent” pitch: each participant pitches one AI role they built. Other people can borrow the agent brief.

---

## Block 6 — Morning 2: Automation + SOPs + Human-in-the-Loop

**Theme:** “Automate repeatable work, not unclear thinking.”

### Outcomes

Participants leave with:

- One selected automation opportunity.
- A written SOP that AI can follow.
- A human approval workflow.

### Teaching Points

- Automation ladder:
  1. Manual with AI help
  2. Checklist
  3. Template
  4. Semi-automated workflow
  5. Fully automated with monitoring
- Why SOPs come before automation.
- Human-in-the-loop design:
  - approve
  - edit
  - reject
  - escalate
- Common automation tools and patterns:
  - forms → sheets → AI summary
  - email/message → classification → draft reply
  - content source → repurposed posts
  - meeting transcript → action items
  - sales data → dashboard/alert

### Activities

1. **Workflow Audit:** pick one repetitive workflow.
2. **SOP Writing:** document trigger, input, steps, output, owner, review rules.
3. **AI SOP Test:** ask AI to execute the SOP with sample data.
4. **Failure Mode Mapping:** what could go wrong?
5. **Approval Design:** decide where humans must review.

### Artifact

- `Automation Opportunity Scorecard`
- One complete SOP
- One human-in-the-loop approval flow

### Community Mechanic

Workflow swap: each pod member gives their SOP to another participant. If the other person cannot follow it, the SOP is not clear enough.

## Block 7 — Afternoon 1: Decision Support, Research, and Evaluation

**Theme:** “AI should help you think better, not just produce more.”

### Outcomes

Participants leave with:

- A research workflow.
- A decision memo template.
- An AI output evaluation checklist.

### Teaching Points

- How to use AI for decisions:
  - clarify decision
  - collect evidence
  - generate options
  - compare tradeoffs
  - identify risks
  - recommend next step
- AI hallucination and verification basics.
- How to ask for citations and source separation.
- How to evaluate output quality:
  - accuracy
  - usefulness
  - specificity
  - actionability
  - brand fit
  - risk
- How to avoid outsourcing judgment.

### Activities

1. **Decision Selection:** each participant chooses a real business decision.
2. **Research Prompt:** create an AI research brief.
3. **Decision Memo:** AI drafts a one-page decision memo.
4. **Red Team Review:** pod members challenge assumptions and risks.
5. **Final Recommendation:** participant states the decision and next action.

### Artifact

- `AI Research Brief Template`
- `One-Page Decision Memo`
- `AI Output Evaluation Checklist`

### Community Mechanic

Board of advisors: pods act as each person’s advisory board. One person presents a decision; the pod asks hard questions.

# Suggested Timing Per Block

Each block can run 90–120 minutes.

## 90-Minute Block Template

- 0–10 min: Story / framing / outcome
- 10–25 min: Teaching + live demo
- 25–55 min: Hands-on build
- 55–70 min: Pair/pod feedback
- 70–82 min: Improve artifact
- 82–90 min: Share one insight / save artifact

## 120-Minute Block Template

- 0–15 min: Story / framing / outcome
- 15–35 min: Teaching + live demo
- 35–75 min: Hands-on build
- 75–95 min: Peer review / pod challenge
- 95–110 min: Revise and polish
- 110–120 min: Public share / capture best examples

  - can help others with

  - Business
  - One AI goal
  - One thing I can help others with

## During Workshop

- Fixed AI Buddy Pods for two days.
- Rotating pair exercises inside pods.
- Shared prompt/library wall.
- Daily reflection posts.
- Public wins board.
- “Ask the room before asking Jedi” rule to build peer connection.

- 30-day accountability rhythm:
  - Week 1: share first implemented workflow
  - Week 2: share one prompt/agent improvement
  - Week 3: share one measurable win
  - Week 4: demo day / community showcase
- Optional office hours.
- Community leaderboard based on contribution, not just success.
- Best participant systems become case studies.

# Facilitator Notes for Jedi

- Start each block with a vivid real-world story, not theory.
- Keep lectures short; the value is in building and feedback.
- Use Thai business examples whenever possible.
- Push specificity: vague business = vague AI output.
- Celebrate small wins publicly.
- Make participants help each other; this builds community and reduces dependency on the instructor.
- Capture testimonials after Block 4 and Block 8, when transformation is felt.
- Use the capstone pitches as both learning reinforcement and community bonding.
