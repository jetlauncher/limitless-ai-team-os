# Hermes Desktop App — Student Prompt Workbook

## How to use
1. Pick one prompt.
2. Replace every [bracketed] part with your business details.
3. Run it in Hermes Desktop.
4. Ask one follow-up: “Make this more practical for my business.”
5. Save the best result into your company notes.

## Quick business profile prompt
> Here is my business context. Remember it for this session only unless I explicitly ask you to save it: Business: [what you sell]. Customer: [who buys]. Price point: [range]. Voice: [tone]. Constraints: [time/team/budget]. Current goal: [goal]. When answering, make recommendations practical for a non-technical business owner.

## Prompt tests

### 1. Research brief
> I run a business that sells [offer] to [audience]. Research the current market for [topic]. Create a one-page owner brief with: key trends, customer pain points, competitor patterns, risks, and 3 recommended actions. Cite sources or explain where the information came from. Keep it practical and non-technical.

### 2. Offer audit
> Act as a brutally practical offer strategist. Here is my offer: [paste offer]. My target customer is [audience]. Audit it for clarity, urgency, proof, differentiation, pricing risk, and objections. Then rewrite the offer in 3 versions: simple, premium, and high-urgency. Finish with 5 experiments I can run in 7 days.

### 3. Sales follow-up
> Here are notes from a sales conversation: [paste notes]. Extract: buyer goals, pains, objections, budget/timing clues, decision process, and next best action. Draft a warm follow-up email in my voice. Then create 3 follow-up reminders if they do not respond.

### 4. SOP builder
> I want to document this process: [describe process]. Turn it into a simple SOP for a new team member. Include: purpose, required inputs, steps, quality checklist, common mistakes, and when to ask for help. Then create a 5-minute training script.

### 5. Customer feedback themes
> Analyze this customer feedback: [paste reviews/messages/survey answers]. Group it into themes, count rough frequency, quote representative examples, identify urgent fixes, and suggest 5 product/marketing changes. Separate facts from interpretation.

### 6. Hiring scorecard
> I am hiring for [role]. Create a hiring scorecard for a non-technical business owner. Include outcomes for the role, must-have skills, interview questions, red flags, trial task, and a simple 1–5 scoring rubric.

### 7. KPI explainer
> Here are my numbers: [paste metrics]. Use a calculation tool if needed. Explain what changed, what likely caused it, what I should investigate, and what actions I should take this week. Show assumptions and formulas clearly.

### 8. Landing page critique
> Analyze this landing page [paste URL or attach screenshot]. Review it for above-the-fold clarity, target customer fit, proof, friction, CTA strength, mobile readability, and trust. Give me the top 10 fixes ranked by expected impact and ease.

### 9. Content repurpose
> Turn this source material into business content: [paste transcript/notes]. Produce: 1 LinkedIn post, 1 email newsletter, 1 carousel outline, 5 short video hooks, and 10 tweet-style insights. Keep the ideas practical for [audience] and preserve my point of view.

### 10. Automation designer
> Help me design a safe Hermes automation for [recurring task]. Ask first: what data source, frequency, output format, delivery channel, and approval rule are needed. Then propose the automation prompt, safety boundaries, and how I should verify the first run.
