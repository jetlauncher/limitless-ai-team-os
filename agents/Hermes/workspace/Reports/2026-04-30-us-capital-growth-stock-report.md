## 2. Methodology

I screened a basket of US-listed companies across your requested sectors and pulled market/fundamental data including:

- latest price
- market capitalization
- forward P/E
- trailing P/E where available
- PEG ratio where available
- price/book
- profit margin
- revenue growth
- earnings growth
- return on equity
- dividend yield
- beta
- 1-year, 6-month, and 3-month price momentum
- analyst mean target upside where available

Then I built a simple composite ranking using:

- **30% growth** — revenue and earnings growth
- **25% quality** — margins and ROE
- **20% momentum** — 1Y/6M/3M price action
- **15% valuation** — forward P/E, PEG, price/book
- **10% analyst upside** — distance to target price

This is not a mechanical buy list. It is a structured way to identify names worth deeper research.

## 3. Market Lens for Capital Gains

For capital gains, we want companies with at least one of these characteristics:

1. **Durable earnings growth** — revenue and EPS can compound for years.
2. **Multiple expansion potential** — market may re-rate the stock higher.
3. **Turnaround catalyst** — bad sentiment improves while fundamentals recover.
4. **Secular growth exposure** — AI, healthcare innovation, power demand, cloud, automation.
5. **Balance-sheet resilience** — ability to survive downturns and keep investing.
6. **Operating leverage** — revenue growth converts into faster earnings growth.

The best stocks usually combine several of these.
