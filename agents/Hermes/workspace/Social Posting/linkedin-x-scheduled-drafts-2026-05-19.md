### 08:30 ICT — X/Twitter — Claude for SMEs is a workflow signal

Claude for Small Business is not just another AI launch.

It is a signal for SME owners:

Stop asking, “What can I ask AI?”

Start asking, “Which repeated workflow should become a company system?”

Pick one this week:
- customer FAQs
- sales follow-ups
- SOP drafts
- monthly sales review

Tool skill matters.
Workflow clarity matters more.

### 10:00 ICT — LinkedIn — The SME AI team is not a chatbot

Claude for Small Business is a useful signal for every founder, even if you do not use Claude.

AI is moving from “chatbot I ask questions” to “working layer inside the company.”

For a Thai SME, that shift is very practical.

Old way:
- owner answers repeated questions
- team rewrites the same proposal
- finance waits until month-end
- knowledge lives in a few people’s heads

New way:
- one company brief
- one FAQ bank from real customer questions
- one sales assistant that drafts replies
- one finance workflow that spots margin and cash risks
- one approval rule for anything sensitive

My preferred starting point is simple: choose one repeated task that costs 5+ hours per week.

Then run a 10-80-10 workflow:

10% human sets context.
80% AI drafts, summarizes, or analyzes.
10% human reviews judgment, risk, and final decision.

The goal is not to make the team look busy with AI.

The goal is to make company knowledge reusable.

### 12:30 ICT — X/Twitter — AI Search rewards clear businesses

Google I/O is another reminder: search is becoming an answer engine.

That changes the work for business owners.

Old SEO: publish broad articles and hope for clicks.

New AI Search: make your business easy to understand.

This week, write 20 FAQs from real customer messages.

Add price range, timeline, service area, proof, and next step.

Clear answers will travel further than vague content.

### 20:30 ICT — X/Twitter — The 10-80-10 AI workflow

Don’t let AI do the whole job.

Let AI do the middle 80%.

10% human: goal, context, constraints.
80% AI: draft, analyze, summarize, compare.
10% human: judgment, risk, approval.

This is how small teams get faster without becoming careless.

AI without review creates risk.
AI with a clear workflow becomes an operating system.
