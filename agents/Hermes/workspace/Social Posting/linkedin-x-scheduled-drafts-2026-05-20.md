### 08:30 X/Twitter — AI agents need business context

AI agents are not useful because they are smarter.

They are useful when they can see the right business context.

FAQ. Pricing. SOPs. Customer history. Weekly goals.

Without context, AI guesses.
With context, AI prepares the next action.

### 10:00 LinkedIn — The AI Team OS for SMEs

The important AI shift from Google I/O and Claude for Small Business is not “better chatbots.”

It is AI moving into the daily operating layer of a company.

For a Thai SME owner, this changes the question.

Old question:
“What AI tool should my team use?”

Better question:
“What work should no longer depend on memory, manual lookup, or the founder answering the same thing again?”

Start with three workflows this week:

• Customer Response OS
Put FAQ, pricing, policies, and good replies in one place. Let AI draft answers. Keep humans approving anything sensitive.

• Daily CEO Brief
Have AI summarize sales, stuck work, customer signals, and decisions needed every morning. Five bullets is enough.

• Proposal Assistant
Give AI your offer, pricing, examples, and rules. Let it draft. Then ask it to review what is risky, missing, or too vague before you send it.

The advantage is not having more tools.

The advantage is fewer decisions living only in someone’s head.

This week, choose one repeated workflow that costs the team more than five hours. Document the inputs, draft the rules, and test AI with human review.

### 12:30 X/Twitter — Claude for Small Business, real use

Claude for Small Business is a signal: AI is moving from personal chat to team workspace.

The first use case is not magic.

Take one proposal template, 10 good examples, pricing rules, and common objections.

Let AI draft.
Let humans make the judgment call.

### 20:30 X/Twitter — 10-80-10 AI workflow

Do not let AI do 100% of important work before you have review systems.

Use 10-80-10:

10% human sets direction
80% AI creates the draft
10% human reviews and decides

This is how small teams get speed without losing taste, accuracy, or control.
