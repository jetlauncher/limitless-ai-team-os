### 3. DeepMind: Decoupled DiLoCo points to cheaper, more resilient frontier training infrastructure
- Source: Google DeepMind, "Decoupled DiLoCo: A new frontier for resilient, distributed AI training" (published Apr 23, 2026): https://deepmind.google/blog/decoupled-diloco/
- Search verification: DuckDuckGo results surfaced DeepMind blog, paper/PDF, and arXiv result for Decoupled DiLoCo.
- Signal: DeepMind proposes dividing large training runs across decoupled compute "islands" with asynchronous data flow, making training more resilient to hardware failure and possible across distant data centers with lower bandwidth; page notes a 12B model trained across four U.S. regions using 2–5 Gbps WAN and more than 20x faster than conventional synchronization methods in that setup.
- Why it matters for Jet: Training infrastructure is moving from one giant perfectly synchronized cluster toward more distributed, fault-tolerant systems. This can eventually lower the cost and broaden who can train or fine-tune serious models.
- Best Jet angle: Strategic explainer: "ทำไม AI รุ่นต่อไปอาจถูกลงและเร็วขึ้น: เมื่อการเทรนไม่ต้องอยู่ในดาต้าเซ็นเตอร์เดียว" — useful for founders planning private/local/industry-specific models.
