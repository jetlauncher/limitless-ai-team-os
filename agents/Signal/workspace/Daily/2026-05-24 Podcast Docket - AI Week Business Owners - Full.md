Prepared for: Jet / Limitless Club

Working title: Stop Asking Which AI Tool To Use

## Executive Summary
This week’s AI news was not mainly about smarter chatbots. It was about AI becoming an operating layer for business: action models, coding agents, enterprise deployment, governed tool access, security, commerce, education, and scientific/creative simulation.

The podcast should frame the shift simply: prompting was the first era of AI; agent operations is the next. Business owners should stop asking only “which AI tool should I use?” and start asking “which workflow in my business should become a supervised AI workflow?”

## Episode Promise
In this episode, we break down the 10 AI shifts every business owner needs to understand this week, and how to turn them into practical advantage instead of hype.

## Suggested Opening Hook
Most business owners are still asking the wrong AI question.

They ask: Which AI tool should I use? Should I use ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Perplexity, Cursor?

But this week’s AI news makes one thing clear: the real question is no longer which tool you use. The real question is: what part of your business are you ready to turn into a supervised AI workflow?

AI is shifting from something that answers questions to something that takes action. It writes code. It runs research. It connects to your CRM. It helps with commerce. It audits security. It assists education. It simulates worlds and scientific problems.

Today, I want to walk you through the 10 things every business owner should know about AI this week.

Business owners should think about AI in five layers:
- Models: Gemini, DeepSeek, Qwen, Cohere, OpenAI, Claude.

- Tools and connectors: APIs, MCP, SaaS integrations, CRM, Teams, Salesforce.

## Practical Action Plan for Business Owners
Step 1: List repeated workflows.
- What do we do every day?
- What do we do every week?
- What work requires reading, summarizing, drafting, checking, or updating?
- What work is important but delayed because nobody has time?

Examples: sales follow-up, content research, customer support replies, meeting summaries, proposal drafting, ad creative analysis, competitor tracking, CRM cleanup, internal reporting, code review, invoice checking, training material creation.

Step 2: Pick one safe workflow.
Choose a workflow that is repeated often, has clear inputs, has clear output standards, does not immediately risk money/legal/customer trust, and can be reviewed by a human.

Step 3: Build the AI workflow.
Define the trigger, input, model/tool, output, human approval, and success metric.

Step 4: Add governance.
Answer: what data can it access, what tools can it use, what actions are blocked, what actions require approval, where logs are stored, and how mistakes are recovered.

Step 5: Turn it into a reusable asset.
Do not leave it as a random prompt. Turn it into an SOP, checklist, prompt template, agent skill, automation, internal dashboard, or training module.
