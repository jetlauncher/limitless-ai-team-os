
## Cycle 1

**Key Findings**
- **Terminology mismatch:** Thai SMEs rarely search for or buy “AI agents.” Market evidence appears under LINE OA automation, generative AI chatbots, RPA, CRM sync, or “digital transformation.”
- **LINE-first operating system:** LINE OA is the de facto workflow hub for Thai micro-SMEs (customer service, bookings, follow-ups, PromptPay reconciliation, CRM updates). Multi-channel (Shopee/Lazada/TikTok) is secondary but growing.
- **Operator economics drive adoption:** Value is measured in owner time saved, missed-chat recovery, response-time reduction, and payment/invoice reconciliation—not generic “conversion lift.”
- **Trust & deployment model:** Thai SMEs prefer “AI assistant to owner/staff” with human-in-the-loop approval. Fully autonomous, customer-facing decision-makers face high friction.
- **Backend vs. frontend framing:** Multi-agent orchestration is a provider-side architecture, not a buyer feature. SMEs buy outcomes (booking automation, inventory sync, Thai-language support), not fleet topology.
- **Distribution reality:** Vendors are LINE OA partners, POS/CRM providers, accounting firms, franchisors, and tourism associations—not generic AI SaaS platforms.

**Evidence & Source Notes**
- Government/Regulatory: DEPA, ETDA, and DBD reports focus on LINE OA adoption and SME digitalization. PDPA risk is driven by practical data handling (chat logs, customer PII, staff uploading private data) rather than formal AI regulation.
- Tech reality: Thai NLP quality, code-switching (Thai/English), polite-register handling, and OCR for Thai invoices/receipts are critical integration hurdles.
- Uncertainty: Official SME AI adoption metrics are fragmented and lagging. “AI agent” usage is often conflated with rule-based chatbots or single-LLM workflows. Multi-agent deployment for micro-SMEs is largely untested; most case studies are enterprise or Western.

**What Changed in the Plan**
- Shifted wedge from “AI agents for Thai SMEs” to “LINE-first AI workflow automation.”
- Replaced broad SME targeting with 4 prioritized verticals: online sellers, hospitality/tourism, beauty/clinics, restaurants.
- Reframed multi-agent orchestration as a backend provider architecture; customer-facing messaging focuses on human-in-the-loop, Thai-language fallback, and PromptPay/CRM sync.
- Added operator economics scoring framework (task frequency, pain/cost, integration difficulty, PDPA risk, Thai NLP failure risk, willingness to pay).
- Aligned distribution strategy with LINE OA vendors, local POS/CRM providers, and industry associations.

**Remaining Unknowns**
- Exact willingness-to-pay thresholds for LINE OA AI automation among Thai micro-SMEs.
- Real-world Thai NLP failure rates for code-switching and polite registers in production.
- Which local CRM/POS systems dominate SME workflows and their API readiness for agent integration.
- Active DEPA/ETDA subsidy or grant programs that could accelerate adoption or offset integration costs.
