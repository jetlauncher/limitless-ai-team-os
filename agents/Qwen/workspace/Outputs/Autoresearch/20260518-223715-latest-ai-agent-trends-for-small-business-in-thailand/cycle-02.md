
## Cycle 2

**Objective**
Map actionable AI agent deployment signals for Thai micro-SMEs, aligning Jet’s AI Team OS agent-fleet architecture with local workflow realities, operator economics, and distribution channels.

**Research Dimensions**
1. **Channel & Workflow Reality:** LINE OA is the de facto OS for Thai micro-SMEs (service, bookings, payments, CRM). Secondary channels: Shopee/Lazada/TikTok Shop. Agents must operate within LINE’s API constraints and Thai business communication norms.
2. **Operator Economics & Trust:** Value is measured in owner time saved, missed-chat recovery, and payment reconciliation—not generic conversion lift. Thai SMEs prefer “AI assistant to staff/owner” with human approval over autonomous decision-making.
3. **Vertical-Specific Friction & Compliance:** Clinics/beauty, hospitality, and tutoring face high PDPA/reputation risk. Restaurants/online sellers prioritize speed and cost. Thai NLP hurdles (code-switching, polite registers, OCR for receipts) dictate integration feasibility.
4. **Distribution & GTM Vectors:** SMEs buy through LINE OA agencies, local POS/CRM providers, accounting firms, and industry associations—not generic AI SaaS. DEPA/ETDA/DBD digitalization grants may offset adoption costs.
