- Post-training is becoming a product lever: OPD, CNA, RLVR, reward models, and trajectory quality are about making AI behavior controllable, not just more impressive.
- Compute access is becoming strategy: OpenAI Guaranteed Capacity + YC credits signal that reliable AI capacity is now startup infrastructure.
- Jet’s bookmark trail strongly aligns with AI Team OS: Claude Managed Agents, Antigravity, Hermes/xurl, Claude Code at scale, and AI creative workflows.

## Top 10 X signals

### 1. Claude Managed Agents moves enterprise agents inside the company perimeter
- Source: @claudeai — [original post](https://x.com/claudeai/status/2056645485696315581)
- What happened: Anthropic announced self-hosted sandboxes and MCP tunnels for Claude Managed Agents.
- Key insight: Production agents need controlled execution environments, private network access, and security defaults, not just better prompts.
- Why Jet should care: This validates the Limitless Club message that real AI adoption needs an operating layer: permissions, environments, data boundaries, and monitoring.
- Blaze angle/hook: “AI agents are not ready for your company until they can work inside your rules.”
- Content format: LinkedIn / YouTube segment / Thai reel

### 2. Gemini 3.5 + Antigravity pushes multi-agent work into mission control
- Source: @GoogleDeepMind — [original post](https://x.com/GoogleDeepMind/status/2056787987774816525)
- What happened: Google DeepMind positioned Gemini 3.5 as a model family for agents and coding; related Antigravity posts show multi-agent mission-control surfaces.
- Key insight: Agent UX is becoming orchestration, not chat: multiple agents, long-running tasks, CLI/SDK, and project-level supervision.
- Why Jet should care: Thai founders need to understand that AI advantage is moving from “using ChatGPT” to “designing delegation systems.”
- Blaze angle/hook: “The next AI interface is not a chat window. It is a control room.”
- Content format: Thai reel / YouTube reaction / carousel

### 3. Nous CNA shows behavior can be edited at circuit level
- Source: @NousResearch — [original post](https://x.com/NousResearch/status/2056778746716107193)
- What happened: Nous released Contrastive Neuron Attribution, identifying and ablating sparse MLP neuron sets tied to behaviors such as refusal.
- Key insight: Post-training may become more surgical: edit narrow behavior gates without broad retraining or damaging general ability.
- Why Jet should care: Future business AI will have configurable behavior policies: when to refuse, escalate, ask, execute, or stop.
- Blaze angle/hook: “The next prompt engineering is behavior engineering.”
- Content format: LinkedIn / carousel

### 4. On-policy distillation joins the post-training canon
- Source: @natolambert — [original post](https://x.com/natolambert/status/2056510299579273447)
- What happened: Nathan Lambert framed on-policy distillation as a lasting post-training class alongside SFT/IFT, RLHF, DPO, and RLVR.
- Key insight: Model improvement is increasingly about training on the model’s own live behavior distribution, not only offline demos or preference pairs.
- Why Jet should care: Companies should collect real workflow traces from their own teams; those traces become the dataset for better agents.
- Blaze angle/hook: “Your company’s AI advantage is hidden in the work your team repeats every day.”
- Content format: X post / LinkedIn

### 5. StitchVM makes reward models cheaper for diffusion alignment
- Source: @gohyojun3 — [original post](https://x.com/gohyojun3/status/2056962799415501111)
- What happened: Hyojun Go shared StitchVM, transferring pixel-space reward models to noisy diffusion latents through model stitching.
- Key insight: Reward-driven generation is getting faster and cheaper by moving evaluation into the latent process instead of repeatedly decoding and scoring.
- Why Jet should care: Creative AI workflows will become more controllable: brands can optimize toward style, quality, safety, and consistency without manual review at every step.
- Blaze angle/hook: “AI creative tools are learning to judge their own drafts before you see them.”
- Content format: YouTube segment / carousel

### 6. OpenAI Guaranteed Capacity turns compute into strategic infrastructure
- Source: @OpenAI — [original post](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2056823271774101907)
- What happened: OpenAI announced Guaranteed Capacity for long-term access to OpenAI compute.
- Key insight: Serious AI adoption is now capacity planning, not only API selection. Reliability of access becomes part of business continuity.
- Why Jet should care: Founders building AI-first operations need a compute plan just like they need payment, CRM, and cloud plans.
- Blaze angle/hook: “If AI runs your workflow, compute is no longer an IT detail. It is business infrastructure.”
