# YouTube → X Longform Article Flow

Reference style: Ruben Hassid-style X Article: long, useful, beginner-friendly, structured like a free guide, with screenshots/examples and a strong save/share mechanic.

## Goal
Turn Jet's existing longform YouTube content into premium X Articles / LinkedIn longform essays that feel like practical founder guides — not transcripts.

## Output formats
- Primary: X Article / longform post
- Secondary: LinkedIn article-style post
- Derivatives: 3 X posts, 1 carousel outline, 3 short reel scripts

## Recommended article structure

### 1. Big simple promise
Formula:
`[Topic] for [Audience].`

Examples:
- AI Workflows for Founders.
- ChatGPT for Thai Business Owners.
- AI Agents for Operators.
- The Founder’s Guide to Using AI Without Creating More Chaos.

### 2. Pattern interrupt hook
Start with a bold observation:
- Most founders are using AI like a toy, not an operating system.
- 95% of businesses don’t have an AI problem. They have a workflow problem.
- If your team only uses ChatGPT for writing, you’re missing most of the leverage.

### 3. Who this is for / not for
Clarifies audience and creates trust.

Example:
`Skip this if you already have documented workflows, clean data, and AI running inside your operations. This is for founders who know AI matters but still don’t know where to start.`

### 4. Why now
Use market signal / Signal intel / recent platform shift.

Questions:
- What changed?
- Why does this matter now?
- What happens if founders ignore it?

### 5. Define the concept simply
Plain-English explainer.

Example sections:
- What is an AI workflow?
- What is an AI agent?
- What is a data fabric?
- What is an AI operating system?

### 6. Compare against what they already know
Make it easy to understand.

Example:
- ChatGPT vs Claude
- AI tool vs AI workflow
- Prompt vs system
- Automation vs delegation
- Content calendar vs Content OS

### 7. Practical framework
Give a memorable model.

Examples:
- The 5-layer AI-readiness stack
- The 3 questions before automating anything
- The AI Workflow Audit
- The Founder AI OS: Input → Context → Tool → Review → Output

### 8. What it is good for
Concrete use cases.

Example:
- summarizing customer calls
- turning videos into content assets
- building decision logs
- drafting sales follow-ups
- analyzing messy spreadsheets

### 9. What it is bad/risky for
Adds credibility.

Example:
- vague tasks
- sensitive data without governance
- fully autonomous actions without review
- facts without verification

### 10. First-week action plan
Beginner-friendly tasks.

Example:
`Pick 3 this week:`
1. Upload one meeting transcript and extract decisions.
2. Turn one sales call into objections + follow-ups.
3. Convert one YouTube video into 5 content assets.
4. Audit one repeated workflow.
5. Create one SOP with AI.

### 11. Jet's operator/pro section
Advanced section at the end.

Example:
`How operators use this differently`
- They don't ask for one output.
- They build repeatable workflows.
- They save templates.
- They connect source material.
- They review outputs with taste.

### 12. Closing CTA
No cheap bait. Strong action.

Examples:
- Save this and run the audit with your team this week.
- Send this to the person in your company responsible for AI adoption.
- Before buying another AI tool, fix the workflow it will depend on.

## YouTube-to-article pipeline

### Step 1 — Source selection
Pick YouTube videos that are:
- evergreen enough to stay relevant
- built around one clear business lesson
- practical, not only motivational
- connected to Jet's offer: AI-readiness, workflows, founder leverage, content systems, business AI adoption

### Step 2 — Transcript extraction
For each YouTube URL:
- fetch transcript
- preserve timestamps
- identify chapter/topic shifts
- extract strong claims, stories, frameworks, examples, and phrases in Jet's voice

### Step 3 — Article angle selection
Do not summarize the video. Reframe it as a guide.

Angle filters:
- What should a founder do differently after reading this?
- What mistake does this correct?
- What framework can we teach?
- What is the sharpest title?

### Step 4 — Build the article skeleton
Use the 12-part structure above.

### Step 5 — Add proof/examples
Pull from:
- YouTube transcript moments
- Signal intel
- Jet's existing business examples
- screenshots/tools where relevant

### Step 6 — Write in Jet's public voice
Rules:
- direct, premium, founder-relevant
- simple sentences
- no generic AI hype
- no “Written by Blaze” attribution
- no overexplaining unless beginner guide intentionally requires it

### Step 7 — Create derivatives
From each article:
- 3 X posts
- 1 LinkedIn post
- 1 carousel outline
- 3 short reel scripts
- 1 CTA/offer bridge

### Step 8 — Schedule with rolling cadence
- Plan 14 days ahead
- Schedule 3–5 days ahead
- Use Signal intel to refresh angles daily
- Ask Jet first if angle feels borderline

## Quality checklist
Before publishing:
- Does the title make a founder want to save it?
- Is the first line strong enough without context?
- Is there a practical framework?
- Does it teach something actionable?
- Is it clearly Jet's authority lane?
- Are all claims either sourced, obvious, or framed as opinion?
- Did we remove assistant attribution from public copy?
