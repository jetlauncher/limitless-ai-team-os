### 4. Async agents need business events, not human babysitting.
Why it matters for founders/business owners: Google’s Gemini API webhooks update is technical, but the business lesson is simple: AI work is becoming long-running and event-driven. Owners should stop imagining AI as a chat window and start designing triggers, callbacks, alerts, and exception handling for jobs that take minutes or hours.
Recommended formats: operator carousel; SOP checklist; internal automation lesson.
Hook ideas: "ถ้า AI ต้องมีคนคอย refresh หน้าจอ ทุก 5 นาที — คุณยังไม่ได้ automate งานจริง" / "Agents are workers. Workers need triggers, status, and handoff rules."
Supporting points: Gemini API webhooks push real-time HTTP POST payloads when long jobs finish; examples include Deep Research, long video generation, and high-volume batch processing; requests are signed following Standard Webhooks.
Source signal: Google Keyword — "Event-Driven Webhooks in the Gemini API" (May 4, 2026).
Jet personalization note: Connect to SOPs and customer conversations: quote generation, lead enrichment, report prep, video creation, and research jobs should notify the responsible person instead of being manually chased.
