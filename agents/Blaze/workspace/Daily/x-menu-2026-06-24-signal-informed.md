## 10 X/Twitter drafts

### 1. Agent control is management (Signal: DeepMind agent control)
AI agents need managers, not cheerleaders.

Before an agent touches customers, money, code, or contracts, define:

• what it can access
• what it cannot decide
• when it must stop
• who approves the final action

The demo is not the system.
The control layer is.

### 2. Team AI beats private prompting (Signal: Claude Tag)
The next AI shift is simple:

From “one smart person using AI”
to “the team working with AI in the same room.”

If your best prompts live in one person’s chat history, your business has no AI memory.

Start by moving one repeated workflow into a shared team process.

### 3. The permission problem (Signal: Snyk Evo)
Most founders ask: “How fast can AI build this?”

Better question:

“What is this AI allowed to touch?”

Codebase? Database? Customer data? Payment flow? Production deploy?

Speed without permissions is not progress.
It is risk with a better UI.

### 4. AI glasses are an operations signal (Signal: Meta Glasses)
AI glasses are not just a creator gadget.

They are a signal that AI is moving closer to real work:

• store inspections
• sales walkthroughs
• staff training
• customer support
• field reports

Start mapping the moments your business should capture before the hardware arrives.

### 5. One workflow first (Signal: AI control roadmap)
If your team is confused by AI, do not add another tool.

Pick one workflow:

• weekly sales follow-up
• customer FAQ
• meeting summary
• content repurposing
• support escalation

Give it an owner, a prompt, a review rule, and a weekly scorecard.

### 6. The founder bottleneck test (Signal: team AI)
A simple founder test:

What question does your team ask you every week because the answer still lives in your head?

That is not a people problem.
That is a business memory problem.

Document it once.
Turn it into an AI-assisted workflow.
Review it every Friday.

### 7. Hands-free content needs SOPs (Signal: Meta Glasses)
Hands-free AI capture will create more content.

But more footage is not the win.

The win is having a simple SOP:
1 problem
1 proof shot
3 useful points
1 next step

Without the SOP, AI glasses just create a bigger camera roll.

### 8. Agent security for non-technical founders (Signal: Snyk Evo)
If you use AI to build websites, automations, or internal tools, add this rule:

No AI-generated code goes live without:

• secret check
• dependency check
• test cases
• human review
• rollback plan

Small businesses do not need enterprise theater.
They need basic discipline.

### 9. The shared room advantage (Signal: Claude Tag)
AI becomes useful when it sees the work, not just the prompt.

A sales room.
A support room.
A content room.
A weekly ops room.

Put AI where decisions already happen.
Then teach it the role, the context, the limits, and the handoff.

### 10. Build trust before scale (Signal: DeepMind control framework)
Do not scale an AI workflow you cannot inspect.

First prove:

• where the answer came from
• when it fails
• what humans approve
• what gets logged
• what improves next week

Trust is not a feeling.
It is a management system.
