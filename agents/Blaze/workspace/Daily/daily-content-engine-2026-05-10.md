### 3 Short Reel Scripts

#### Short Reel 1: Receipts Before Access
Hook: Stop giving AI deeper access just because it sounds smart.

Script: In business, a confident answer is not proof. If an AI replies to customers, writes code, qualifies leads, or touches spend, it needs receipts: logs, sources, tests, approval points, and outcome checks. #### Short Reel 2: The Demo Trap
Hook: A great AI demo can still become an operational risk.

Script: Demos reward fluency. Operations reward reliability. The question is not “Did the AI sound impressive?” The question is “Can we verify the task was done correctly, repeatedly, and safely?” If your AI cannot show evidence, don’t let it touch a critical system.

CTA: Build receipts before you build automation.

#### Short Reel 3: The New AI KPI
Hook: Your AI should not just answer. It should prove.

Script: The first wave of AI was about prompting. The next wave is about proof. Test results. Audit logs. Source trails. Human approval rules. Escalation paths. Founders who build these systems will use AI deeper than teams still relying on vibes.

CTA: Don’t ask what AI can do. Ask what it can prove.

### 3 Stronger Camera-Ready Reel Scripts

#### Strong Reel 1: AI Needs Receipts
Hook: The most dangerous AI in your company is not the one that gets things wrong. It’s the one that gets things wrong confidently.

4. If AI writes code, you need tests, diffs, permissions, and rollback paths.
5. If AI qualifies leads, you need criteria, confidence, and conversion feedback.

7. The next phase of AI is not prompt engineering. It is receipt engineering.

CTA: Before you scale an AI agent, define the receipt it must leave behind.

#### Strong Reel 2: Stop Trusting AI Reasoning Alone
Hook: AI reasoning is not quality control.

3. Founders need to separate narrative from evidence.
4. Don’t ask, “Does the reasoning sound logical?” Ask, “What test proves this worked?”
5. Build evaluation sets from real mistakes: bad leads, wrong customer replies, failed campaigns, broken handoffs.
6. Only give AI more access after it passes clearer checks.
7. Trust should be earned by outcomes, not fluency.

CTA: If your AI cannot be evaluated, it should not be automated.

If AI is going to touch real parts of the business — customers, code, leads, budgets, refunds, reporting, or internal decisions — the question changes.

Not: “Can the AI do this?”

But: “Can the AI prove what it did, why it did it, and whether it worked?”

That means every serious AI workflow needs receipts:

- source logs for customer-facing answers
- tests and diffs for code changes
- qualification criteria for leads
- confidence thresholds for decisions
- human approval points for high-risk actions
- budget limits for spend
- audit trails for anything that affects money, customers, or reputation
- outcome checks that compare AI work against real business results

The first wave of AI adoption was about prompts.

The next wave is about accountability.

Founders who understand this will be able to give AI deeper access because they will have stronger controls around it.

Founders who skip this step will confuse speed with leverage.

And speed without verification is not leverage.

It is risk moving faster than your company can see.

Before scaling an AI agent, ask one simple question:

What receipt does this system leave behind?

If the answer is unclear, the workflow is not ready for deeper access.
