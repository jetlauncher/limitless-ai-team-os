## 10 X/Twitter drafts

1. Terminal agents
Most teams still treat AI like a smarter chat box.

The better teams are putting it where work actually happens:
terminal, docs, CRM, dashboards, support inbox.

If AI is outside the workflow, people will forget to use it.

2. Local AI
Local AI is becoming interesting again.

Not because every company needs to run models on a laptop.

Because some work should never leave the business:
customer lists, margin data, contracts, internal SOPs.

The next AI question is not “which model?”
It is “which data stays inside?”

3. Context layer
The real AI bottleneck is not prompts.

It is context.

If your company knowledge lives in Slack, Line, Google Drive, Notion, old PDFs, and one manager’s brain, AI will only give you polished guesses.

Fix the memory layer before blaming the model.

4. Enterprise AI platforms
Enterprise AI is getting serious.

Azure, NVIDIA, Anthropic, AWS, LangChain, open models.
Everyone is building the rails.

For founders, the lesson is simple:
do not wait for the perfect tool.

Design the workflow first.
The platform can change later.

5. AI memory
Most businesses do not have an AI problem.
They have a memory problem.

The same questions get answered again.
The same mistakes repeat.
The same decisions depend on one person.

AI works better when the business can remember.

6. Managing agents
An AI agent is not magic staff.

It still needs:
• a clear brief
• access to the right files
• a definition of done
• review gates
• a manager who checks the output

Bad delegation does not become good delegation because AI is involved.

7. Bug reports for AI
One underrated AI skill:
writing better bug reports.

Not “this is broken.”

Try:
• what happened
• what should have happened
• exact steps
• screenshot or log
• where to look first

Agents perform better when the human gives clean signals.

8. Open model cost pressure
Open models are getting good enough to change the cost conversation.

Not for every task.
Not for every company.

But enough that founders should start splitting work into:
cheap automation, sensitive work, high-judgment work.

One model for everything is lazy architecture.

9. Content measurement
Video AI research is moving toward measuring what actually changes audience behavior.

Good.

Most content teams still judge by taste, likes, and vibes.

The smarter question:
which hook, scene, proof, or offer made the buyer pay attention?

10. Communication layer
Agents will not help much if they cannot notify the right person at the right time.

The quiet win is not “more automation.”

It is a business nervous system:
when this happens, tell this person, with this context, before it becomes expensive.
