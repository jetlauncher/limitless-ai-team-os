## 10 English X/Twitter post drafts

1. **Terminal agents need process first** (Signal: Gemini CLI)

The most useful AI agents will not look like apps.

They will live inside the workflows your team already uses.

The founder's job is not to chase every new agent.

It is to pick one repeatable workflow, define the expected output, add a review gate, and measure whether the work actually improved.

2. **Do not trust one good demo** (Signal: agentic AI eval research)

A good AI demo tells you almost nothing.

The real question is:

Can it do the same task 50 times, across messy edge cases, without creating work for your team?

Before you deploy an agent, build a small eval set from your real sales, support, finance, and ops cases.

3. **Prompt injection is now an ops problem** (Signal: agent data injection attacks)

If your AI agent reads email, websites, files, or customer messages, it is not just “answering prompts.”

It is consuming untrusted data.

That means security is now part of the workflow design.

Permissions, approvals, logging, and secret protection are not technical extras. They are management basics.

4. **Local AI is a control decision** (Signal: LocalAI)

Local AI is not only about saving money.

It is about control.

Which data can leave the company?
Which tasks need speed?
Which workflows need privacy?
Which outputs still need a human owner?

For some businesses, the next AI decision is not “better model.”

It is “where should this work run?”

5. **Context is the new company asset** (Signal: RAGFlow + agent memory)

Most businesses do not have an AI problem.

They have a context problem.

The knowledge lives in old chats, staff memory, scattered docs, and the founder's head.

RAG and agent memory matter because they turn that messy knowledge into something the business can actually reuse.

6. **Agent platforms need owners** (Signal: Langflow / agent workflow tools)

Workflow builders make AI agents easier to create.

They do not make them easier to manage.

Every agent still needs:

• one owner
• one job
• one success metric
• one failure rule
• one review rhythm

Without that, you do not have automation.

You have invisible risk.

7. **Creative AI is a testing engine** (Signal: video feature research + Meta Muse)

AI creative tools are not important because they make prettier images.

They are important because they make testing faster.

A founder can test 5 hooks, 3 offers, 3 visuals, and 2 proof angles before lunch.

The edge is not more content.

The edge is faster learning.

8. **Enterprise AI is moving to governance** (Signal: NVIDIA / Azure / Bedrock enterprise platforms)

Big companies are not asking, “Can AI do this?” anymore.

They are asking:

Who approved it?
Where did the data go?
What happens when it fails?
How do we know it worked?

SMEs should copy that mindset early, just with lighter tools.

9. **Code agents expose weak systems** (Signal: Formal Disco / coding agents)

AI coding agents reduce the cost of writing code.

They do not reduce the cost of unclear requirements.

If your specs are vague, your tests are weak, and nobody owns QA, faster coding just creates faster confusion.

The real upgrade is not the agent.

It is the operating discipline around it.

10. **Robotics starts with workflow mapping** (Creative Director secondary: Robostral Navigate)

Do not start robotics by shopping for robots.

Start by listing work that is:

• repeated daily
• visible by camera
• limited to one area
• low risk if it fails
• measurable in time saved

The best automation projects usually begin as boring workflow maps.
