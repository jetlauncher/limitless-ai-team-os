## Micro-upgrades

### 1. Unify Story Highlight covers into branded minimal icons

**Exact change:** Replace the current random story thumbnail screenshots with a simple branded icon system. 6–8 highlights max. Use a solid `#17181A` (near-black) circle background with a minimalist bronze `#94764A` icon or word label in a clean sans-serif. Examples: "Courses" (book icon), "Events" (stage icon), "AI Tools" (bolt icon), "Testimonials" (star icon).

**Why it increases luxury association:** Right now the highlight row looks like an uncurated archive — random frames from different events and posts. Every luxury brand curates even its smallest touchpoints. Consistent highlight covers signal intentionality: "this account is designed, not dumped into." It also creates a recognizable navigation menu that new visitors can scan instantly.

**Implementation note:** Create 6–8 highlight covers in the Midnight Luxe Editorial system. 1080×1080 circles, black background, bronze icon/label, white accent lettering. Re-upload stories to each highlight with the new cover. Takes ~30 minutes.

**Priority:** HIGH — the first thing visitors see under the bio. It's the lowest-hanging visual upgrade on the entire profile.

### 2. Add "Founder, Limitless Club" to the Display Name line

**Exact change:** Current display name: "Jedi | AI Growth Coach & Business Consultant" → "Jedi Trinupab | Limitless Club"

### 3. Tighten the bio from 4 lines to 3 with editorial hierarchy

**Exact change:** Current bio is functional but reads like a bulleted resume. Restructure into a 3-line editorial hierarchy:

```
Founder, Limitless Club
AI Scale Coach for 7-figure+ founders
2,000+ students trained → limitlessclub.com
```

Drop "Entrepreneur" from the first line (it's implied by everything else and doesn't differentiate). Move the CTAs into a single clean line.

**Why it increases luxury association:** Luxury brands say less and mean more. "Entrepreneur" is the least specific word in the bio — it communicates nothing that the rest doesn't already cover with higher credibility. The 3-line version has a clear arc: who → what → proof + funnel. Every word earns its slot.

**Implementation note:** Profile > Edit Bio > Edit. Draft for Jet approval. Test for 2 weeks, revert if engagement drops.

**Priority:** MEDIUM — 2-minute edit, but positioning-sensitive. Worth Jet's approval.

### 4. Introduce a carousel cover template in the Midnight Luxe Editorial system

**Exact change:** The grid currently has no visual template system for carousels — every cover looks different. Create a reusable carousel cover format:

- **Background:** `#17181A` black
- **Header:** Small "Limitless Club" in uppercase tracking-wide (system mark at top)
- **Headline:** Editorial serif in `#E2D7C8` warm ivory, 1–2 lines max
- **Accent:** Single thin bronze `#94764A` horizontal rule
- **Footer:** "@jeditrinupab" in small sans-serif
- **Optional crosshair gridlines** at 20% opacity for depth

Apply this to 100% of future carousel posts. Reel covers get a simpler version: same background, just the headline text.

**Why it increases luxury association:** Without a template, the grid reads as "posts happening" rather than "a publication publishing." The difference between a magazine and a blog is systematization. When someone scrolls the grid, they should feel the brand before they read the post. This creates visual rhythm — the eye recognizes the Limitless Club pattern at 200ms, and trust follows recognition.

**Implementation note:** Can be built in Canva or Figma as a master template. Each new carousel just swaps the headline. I already have the Midnight Luxe Editorial system designed from prior work.

**Priority:** HIGH — requires design setup upfront, but compounds forever. Every future carousel becomes a brand asset, not just content.

**Priority:** LOW — long-game, not a surface edit. Flag for awareness.
