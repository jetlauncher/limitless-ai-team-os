- Focus surface: Instagram `instagram.com/jeditrinupab`
- Rotation rationale: yesterday’s audit was YouTube, so today is Instagram.

## First-impression read
Jet’s Instagram already has strong authority signals: verified account, 92.7K followers, professional avatar, business/AI positioning, proof bullets, and a grid with real-world training/social-proof imagery. The luxury gap is mainly system discipline. The first impression mixes premium cues — dark profile UI, sharp portrait, strong live event proof — with more casual creator-growth cues: emoji-heavy bio, highlight names with mixed languages/emojis, inconsistent highlight cover treatments, and grid covers that jump between event photo collage, talking-head Reels, tool screenshots, cartoon/AI mascots, yellow text, and white text blocks. The account feels active and credible; the 1% opportunity is to make it feel more deliberately curated and expensive.

## Micro-upgrades

### 1) Rewrite the bio into a calmer executive proof stack
- **Exact change:** For Jet approval, replace the emoji-led bio with a more editorial structure. Draft option:
  ```text
  AI Growth Coach for 7–8 figure founders
  Forbes TH AI Thought Leader
  1,000+ students • 3,000M+ revenue impact
  B2B/B2C strategy • AI systems • corporate training
  Start here ↓
  ```
  Thai-first option:
  ```text
  AI Coach สำหรับเจ้าของธุรกิจ 7–8 หลัก
  Forbes TH AI Thought Leader
  ลูกศิษย์ 1,000+ • ยอดขายรวม 3,000M+
  AI Systems • Strategy • Corporate Training
  เริ่มต้นที่นี่ ↓
  ```
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Luxury positioning relies on proof, specificity, and restraint. The current rocket/trophy/fire/money emoji stack communicates energy, but the icons reduce executive polish. A calmer proof stack makes Jet feel more like a premium advisor than a creator selling urgency.
- **Quick implementation note:** Keep the same conversion logic: who it is for, authority proof, revenue proof, what he does, and the free-start CTA. Only remove visual noise and tighten hierarchy.
- **Priority:** High

### 2) Standardize highlight covers into a bronze-on-charcoal editorial set
- **Exact change:** Create matching highlight covers with:
  - Background: `#17181A` or graphite.
  - Thin bronze ring/accent: `#94764A`.
  - Warm ivory label/icon: `#E2D7C8`.
  - One style of icon or serif initials only.
  - Suggested visible set order: `Start Here`, `AI Workshop`, `Corporate`, `Results`, `Testimonials`, `Events`, `Credentials`, `Services`.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** The visible highlights currently use mixed photo thumbnails and labels such as `เรียนรู้ฟรี😍`, `Corp. Training`, `AI Workshop`, `บรรยากาศคลาส`, `Testimonials`, `Events`. Mixed covers create proof, but not brand memory. A unified cover system instantly makes the profile feel curated and institution-grade.
- **Quick implementation note:** Do not delete any highlight content. Only update covers and possibly rename labels for cleaner first-read hierarchy. Jet should approve names before live edits.
- **Priority:** High

### 3) Pin/grid-first row: treat the top 3 posts as an editorial storefront
- **Exact change:** Keep the pinned proof post if it matters, but make the first row visually intentional:
  1. Left: flagship proof / community scale.
  2. Middle: Jet authority portrait with a clean Thai/English promise.
  3. Right: high-value thought leadership Reel cover.
  Add a shared footer or small top label: `LIMITLESS CLUB` + content category.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** The first row currently has strong material but three different visual languages: event collage with huge “21 รุ่น”, Jet portrait with cropped Thai type, and an English Reel cover with large white text. A storefront row should tell a premium buyer: “this is the world, the expert, and the value.”
- **Quick implementation note:** This can be done prospectively by designing the next 3 covers as a coherent sequence, not by immediately replacing old posts.
- **Priority:** High

### 4) Create a Reel cover restraint rule: one headline, one accent, one footer
- **Exact change:** For every new Reel cover, use:
  - 3–6 word headline max.
  - One accent color only: bronze for premium authority, red/yellow only for high-conflict alerts.
  - Consistent footer: `Limitless Club • @jeditrinupab`.
  - Jet face crop locked to one side or centered, not changing randomly.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Visible covers include English block text, OpenClaw mascot, Codex screenshot, event clips, and varied typography. The content range is valuable, but the cover system needs a premium spine so tool/news content does not cheapen the overall grid.
- **Quick implementation note:** Apply only to the next 10 Reels first. Track whether visual consistency improves profile feel without hurting native performance.
- **Priority:** Medium

### 5) Rename one casual highlight label that weakens the premium surface
- **Exact change:** For Jet approval, change `เรียนรู้ฟรี😍` to one of:
  - `Free Training`
  - `เริ่มเรียนฟรี`
  - `Start Here`
  - `AI Starter`
- **Why it increases luxury association:** The heart-eyes emoji makes the profile feel friendly, but it is less aligned with premium founder-media. A cleaner label keeps the free entry point while making the profile feel more expensive.
- **Quick implementation note:** This is a low-effort text-only cleanup, but should be paired with the cover system so the highlight row becomes a conversion asset, not just an archive.
- **Priority:** Medium

## Today’s recommended 1% upgrade
**Standardize the Instagram highlight row into a bronze-on-charcoal editorial system, starting with cleaner covers and one renamed casual label (`เรียนรู้ฟรี😍` → `Start Here` / `เริ่มเรียนฟรี`).**

## Why this is the best 1% move
- It is highly visible above the grid and affects every profile visit before the user scrolls.
- It preserves all existing proof/content while making the surface feel more curated, premium, and brand-owned.
- It is a small asset-system upgrade: no offer changes, no posting risk, no algorithm dependency.
