# Brand Luxury 1% Live Audit — YouTube — 2026-07-01

## Scope
- Focus surface: YouTube `youtube.com/@jeditrinupab`
- Rotation rationale: 2026-06-30 audit was Instagram, so today rotates to YouTube.
- Live access: public logged-out YouTube channel loaded successfully. No login, edits, posting, scheduling, publishing, boost/ads, payment UI, or profile controls were touched.

## First-impression read
Jet's YouTube has strong authority signals: 104K subscribers, 1.3K videos, a polished dark portrait avatar, a founder/AI niche that is immediately legible, and high-performing recent educational uploads. The current homepage feels credible and energetic, but not yet as expensive as Limitless Club can feel. The premium gap is not credibility; it is visual restraint and system consistency across the banner, thumbnails, and metadata.

The channel banner is the strongest luxury asset on the surface: black base, Jet in dark wardrobe, clear AI category statement, proof/event photos, and Limitless Club mark. However, the red Subscribe button graphic and social icon cluster pull it back toward classic YouTube growth-hack energy. The recent thumbnail row is high-converting and readable, but uses multiple bright palettes, stickers, shocked expressions, arrows, confetti, and large percentage badges. That supports clicks, but dilutes luxury association when viewed as a storefront.

## Visible observations
- **Banner:** Strong black editorial base and proof imagery. Main Thai claim is bold and clear. Limitless Club mark is visible. Red subscribe button + bell/cursor graphic feels less premium than the rest of the banner.
- **Avatar:** Premium and aligned: dark background, black wardrobe, direct eye contact, sharp lighting. Keep as anchor.
- **Homepage first row:** Recent videos communicate value quickly, but the system varies: red/white milestone confetti, green money/ChatGPT, orange 95%, bright token/arrow card, multi-logo comparison cover. The row feels active and useful, less curated/luxury.
- **Titles:** Strong educational clarity. Several titles use `[Full Course]`, which signals value but can feel utilitarian. A more editorial label system could preserve clarity while increasing premium feel.
- **Popular row:** Strong proof from 103K/84K/60K+ views. The visible thumbnails underneath appear less image-loaded in the screenshot, giving the page a calmer premium rhythm, but the top row dominates first impression.
- **Brand memory:** AI/founder category is clear. The missing premium cue is a repeated luxury frame: masthead, restrained accent, footer lockup, consistent typography, and fewer decorative effects.

## Micro-upgrades

### 1) Replace the banner's red Subscribe graphic with a quiet editorial CTA block
- **Exact change:** Draft a banner variant that removes the large red `SUBSCRIBE` button, bell, cursor, and social icon cluster. Replace with a small ivory/bronze footer line: `LIMITLESS CLUB • AI SYSTEMS FOR FOUNDERS` and `@jeditrinupab` / `YouTube: Jedi Trinupab`.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Red buttons and cursor graphics feel like tactical YouTube marketing. A quiet footer lockup feels more like a premium media brand and lets the black/bronze Limitless Club identity lead.
- **Quick implementation note:** Keep the same banner structure, Jet portrait, proof photos, and main claim. Only simplify the right-side CTA area for Jet approval.
- **Priority:** High

### 2) Create a “premium first-row thumbnail rule” for the next 3 uploads
- **Exact change:** For the next three thumbnails, use one consistent base: graphite/black background, ivory headline, one bronze accent, one composed Jet cutout/photo, and a small `LIMITLESS CLUB` or `JEDI` masthead. Avoid more than one bright accent color per cover.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** The top row is the YouTube storefront. A consistent row signals a controlled premium channel rather than a stream of tactical one-off videos.
- **Quick implementation note:** Do not reduce click clarity; keep big readable Thai outcomes, but remove extra stickers, confetti, arrows, and competing color blocks.
- **Priority:** High

### 3) Turn `[Full Course]` into an editorial series badge
- **Exact change:** Keep the title benefit, but visually treat course uploads with a small thumbnail badge: `FIELD GUIDE`, `FULL COURSE`, or `LIMITLESS GUIDE` in a consistent bronze/ivory capsule. Example title format draft: `Full Course: สร้าง Personal Brand บน YouTube ด้วย AI` instead of bracket-heavy styling.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Brackets feel platform-native and tactical; a named series/badge feels like a premium education property.
- **Quick implementation note:** Test on future uploads first. No need to rename past videos without approval.
- **Priority:** Medium-High

### 4) Introduce a thumbnail “one proof object only” rule
- **Exact change:** Each thumbnail gets one dominant proof/object: Jet face, product screenshot, money/result, or logo cluster — not several at once. For comparison/tool videos, reduce logo piles to 3–4 icons max inside a controlled panel.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Luxury design relies on hierarchy. Fewer objects make the promise feel more confident and expensive.
- **Quick implementation note:** Use the current high-performing `AI Agent` and `25 นาที` concepts as reference, then calm the surrounding effects.
- **Priority:** Medium

### 5) Add a recurring footer/masthead lockup to new thumbnails
- **Exact change:** Add a tiny repeated system: top-left `LIMITLESS CLUB` or `JEDI AI SYSTEMS`; bottom-right `@jeditrinupab`; one thin bronze divider line. Keep it small enough not to hurt CTR.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Repetition builds brand memory. Viewers should recognize Jet's channel before reading the title.
- **Quick implementation note:** Start with new long-form videos and Full Course covers only; Shorts can stay more native/raw.
- **Priority:** Medium
