# Brand Luxury 1% Live Audit — YouTube — 2026-07-05

## Scope
- Focus surface: YouTube `youtube.com/@jeditrinupab`
- Rotation rationale: 2026-07-04 audit was Instagram, so today rotates to YouTube.
- Live access: Public logged-out YouTube channel homepage loaded successfully. Visual first-screen audit completed via browser screenshot/vision. No sign-in, editing, posting, scheduling, publishing, ads, payment, or profile controls were touched.

## First-impression read
Jet's YouTube channel already has strong authority signals: 108K subscribers, 1.3K videos, clear AI/business focus, a professional portrait avatar, active recent uploads, and visible traction on recent videos. The current first impression feels energetic, tactical, and creator-led.

The luxury gap is visual restraint. The banner and thumbnails use high-contrast YouTube growth cues — red Subscribe button, bright yellows/oranges, large question marks, busy photo collages, bold glow/outline type, and many competing thumbnail systems. This likely helps click-through, but it slightly reduces the “premium founder-media / expensive advisory” association Limitless Club wants to compound.

## Visible observations
- **Banner:** Strong message (`คนไทย 1,000,000 คนต้องเก่ง AI`) and proof photos, but the red subscribe graphic, hand cursor, social icons, collage density, and mixed logo/photo elements make the top feel more creator-promo than luxury institution.
- **Avatar:** The circular portrait is one of the strongest premium cues: dark background, black jacket, calm expression, editorial headshot quality. It is more luxurious than the surrounding thumbnail/banner system.
- **Homepage hierarchy:** Channel name and subscriber proof are clear. Description line starts with a casual training CTA and emoji (`👉`), which is effective but less premium than an editorial mission/proof line.
- **Recent thumbnails:** Consistent in topic and Jet presence, but visually loud: yellow/orange type, large facial expressions, question marks, multiple color backgrounds, app logos, percentage bubbles, and inconsistent footer/logo placement.
- **Title packaging:** `[Full Course]` is a strong recurring asset. However, recent titles mix English/Thai and tool names in a utilitarian way. There is room for a more editorial series architecture.

## Micro-upgrades

### 1) Make the banner feel like an institution, not a subscribe ad
- **Exact change:** Draft a quieter banner variant: charcoal/black base, one hero portrait of Jet, small Limitless Club mark, one mission line (`คนไทย 1,000,000 คนต้องเก่ง AI`) and one restrained proof line (`AI for founders • operators • teams`). Remove the red Subscribe button graphic, hand cursor, social icons, and reduce collage photos to either 2 small proof frames or none.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Luxury channels do not need to visually beg for subscription. Removing red promo UI and clutter creates confidence, negative space, and a more expensive media-house feel.
- **Quick implementation note:** Draft only for Jet approval. Use #17181A background, #E2D7C8 text, rare #94764A accent line. Keep the mission line large; keep proof photos small and documentary-style.
- **Priority:** High

### 2) Add a “premium course” thumbnail footer system
- **Exact change:** For future long-form/course thumbnails, add a consistent bottom metadata strip: `LIMITLESS CLUB • FULL COURSE` or `AI OPERATING SYSTEM • LESSON 01`, plus `@jeditrinupab` in small warm ivory text. Keep it identical across videos.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** A repeated footer turns individual videos into a recognizable premium library, not disconnected YouTube uploads. It builds brand memory without reducing hook clarity.
- **Quick implementation note:** Start with new uploads only. Use a thin bronze divider and charcoal translucent footer; avoid redesigning the archive immediately.
- **Priority:** High

### 3) Reduce thumbnail color vocabulary to one controlled palette per series
- **Exact change:** For the next 5 uploads, keep the hook text but limit colors to charcoal/navy base + ivory/white type + one bronze or electric-blue AI accent. Avoid simultaneous yellow + orange + red + green + app-color chaos in one row.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Premium brands use color scarcity. A restrained palette makes the channel feel curated while still allowing one AI-tech accent for energy.
- **Quick implementation note:** Build 2 templates: `Course/Authority` = charcoal/ivory/bronze; `Tool Battle/News` = dark navy/white/electric blue. Do not mix both systems in the same thumbnail.
- **Priority:** Medium-High

### 4) Turn `[Full Course]` into an editorial series label
- **Exact change:** Keep `[Full Course]` in the YouTube title if it performs, but make the thumbnail label more elegant: `FULL COURSE` small top-left, with the main Thai hook cleaner and shorter. Example: title can remain `[Full Course] วิธีการใช้ AI Agent เพื่อขยายทีม`; thumbnail label becomes `FULL COURSE / AI TEAM SYSTEM`.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Editorial labels signal a structured curriculum. Brackets in titles are useful for search; refined labels in thumbnails make the product feel more premium.
- **Quick implementation note:** Apply to future covers and optionally A/B test against the current loud style.
- **Priority:** Medium

### 5) Upgrade the channel description’s first visible line from CTA-first to mission-first
- **Exact change:** Draft a calmer first line before the Line OA CTA: `AI systems for Thai founders, operators, and teams.` / `ช่วยเจ้าของธุรกิจไทยสร้างระบบ AI ที่ใช้ได้จริง` then CTA on the next line.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Mission-first positioning feels more premium than link-first selling. The CTA remains, but the first impression becomes strategic.
- **Quick implementation note:** Draft for Jet approval only; do not edit live. Keep the link, but remove emoji from the first visible line if possible.
- **Priority:** Medium

## Today's recommended 1% upgrade
**Draft a quieter YouTube banner system that removes the red Subscribe graphic/hand cursor/social icon cluster and replaces it with an institutional Limitless Club mission banner.**

Why this is the best move today:
- The banner is the largest first-impression asset on the channel.
- It can raise perceived value without touching content strategy or upload cadence.
- It aligns the strong avatar/authority proof with Limitless Club’s premium founder-media direction.
