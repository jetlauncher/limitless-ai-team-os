# Brand Luxury 1% Live Audit — YouTube — 2026-06-25

## Scope
- Focus surface: YouTube `youtube.com/@jeditrinupab`
- Rotation rationale: 2026-06-24 audit was Instagram, so today rotates to YouTube.
- Live access: public logged-out YouTube channel homepage loaded successfully. Inspected banner, avatar, header/bio preview, nav, homepage video row, popular row, AI playlist row, Shorts row preview, and posts preview.
- No public assets were edited, posted, scheduled, boosted, or changed.

## First-impression read
The channel has strong creator authority: 91.2K subscribers, 1.3K videos, a professional portrait avatar, active recent uploads, and visible proof of audience momentum. The premium gap is not trust; it is visual restraint. The banner and thumbnails lean toward energetic YouTube growth cues — bright red Subscribe button, cursor/bell graphics, yellow/orange text, excited face expressions, heavy outlines, and mixed thumbnail systems. This likely helps click-through, but it slightly weakens the Limitless Club luxury association. The best 1% move is to keep performance energy while introducing a quieter recurring “founder-media seal” on recent and future thumbnails.

## Visual observations
- **Banner:** Black base and Jet portrait are on-brand. Group/classroom photos add proof. However, the large red `SUBSCRIBE` block, cursor, social icons, and mixed photo collage make the banner feel YouTube-marketer rather than high-class founder-media.
- **Avatar:** Strong. Dark portrait, black clothing, direct eye contact, clean circular crop. It is more premium than most surrounding assets and should remain the anchor.
- **Header/bio preview:** Simple and credible, but the visible Thai line leads with workshop Line OA and `...more`. Practical, not luxurious. The above-fold association is “contact/workshop” more than “premium AI founder media.”
- **Homepage first row thumbnails:** Strong clarity and high energy. But the row mixes blue AI, yellow/orange token, grey comparison, neon green local AI, red/orange emphasis, circular percentage badge, multiple pose cutouts, and heavy shadows. It feels useful and clickable, but less expensive-looking.
- **Typography/title system:** Titles are practical and search-friendly. Thumbnail typography varies in weight, outline, color, and language emphasis. There is no small recurring Limitless Club masthead across covers.
- **Premium strongest assets:** The avatar, black banner base, Jet in black blazer, and large simple Thai banner statement are closest to Midnight Luxe.

## Micro-upgrades

### 1) Add a tiny “Limitless Club seal” to all future YouTube thumbnails
- **Exact change:** Standardize a small top-right or bottom-left mark: `LIMITLESS CLUB` in warm ivory on charcoal, with one thin bronze hairline. Keep it under 4–5% of the thumbnail area.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** A repeated restrained mark creates brand memory without lowering CTR or making thumbnails feel like ads. It makes a high-volume channel feel intentionally published.
- **Quick implementation note:** Apply only to new uploads first. Do not retrofit past thumbnails until a few new ones prove the look.
- **Priority:** High

### 2) Redesign the channel banner to remove loud subscription graphics
- **Exact change:** Draft a cleaner banner: black/charcoal base, Jet portrait, 2–3 proof photos in a quiet editorial grid, headline `คนไทย 1,000,000 คนต้องเก่ง AI`, Limitless Club logo, and a subtle CTA line such as `Subscribe for AI systems, agents, and business growth` instead of the red Subscribe button/cursor.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Luxury brands do not visually beg for the click. Removing red/button/cursor cues makes the top of the channel feel more expensive while preserving the mission line and proof.
- **Quick implementation note:** Draft for Jet approval only; do not change live banner.
- **Priority:** High

### 3) Create a two-lane thumbnail system: “Tactical” vs “Editorial”
- **Exact change:** Keep high-energy faces and bold Thai text for tutorial/search videos, but use a calmer black/bronze/ivory editorial style for authority pieces, full courses, and founder-thesis videos.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Not every video needs the same volume. A premium channel can preserve CTR on tactical videos while making flagship content look collectible and high-value.
- **Quick implementation note:** Tag future concepts at planning stage: `TACTICAL CTR` or `EDITORIAL AUTHORITY`. Editorial covers should use fewer colors, fewer objects, and one strong title block.
- **Priority:** Medium-High

### 4) Calm the visible channel description line
- **Exact change:** Draft a more premium first visible sentence for approval, e.g. `Limitless Club: AI systems, agents, and growth strategy for modern founders.` Then keep the Line OA/workshop CTA in the expanded description or link area.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** The first line currently sells access. A premium first line should establish category ownership before conversion.
- **Quick implementation note:** This is a live profile edit; draft only for Jet approval.
- **Priority:** Medium

### 5) Use a consistent metadata strip for long-form/course uploads
- **Exact change:** For `[Full Course]` videos, add a restrained bottom metadata strip: `FULL COURSE`, duration, and topic category in ivory/bronze on charcoal.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Courses look more valuable when packaged like an editorial product series rather than one-off uploads.
- **Quick implementation note:** Start with the next full-course upload. Avoid adding too much text; the strip should support hierarchy, not compete with the hook.
- **Priority:** Medium

## Today’s recommended 1% upgrade
**Draft and begin using a tiny `LIMITLESS CLUB` thumbnail seal for future YouTube uploads — no live changes yet.**

Why this is the best move today:
- It is small, fast, and compounds across every new upload.
- It upgrades the channel’s brand memory without risking current top-performing thumbnail language.
- It bridges YouTube CTR energy with the Midnight Luxe premium system.

## Draft direction for Jet approval
- Seal: `LIMITLESS CLUB` uppercase, warm ivory `#E2D7C8`, 1px bronze `#94764A` line, charcoal backing `#17181A` at 80–90% opacity.
- Placement: default bottom-left unless it conflicts with YouTube duration; alternate top-right.
- Size: small enough to feel like a publisher mark, not a logo slap.
- Avoid: red CTA graphics, oversized logos, extra social icons, decorative glows around the seal.
