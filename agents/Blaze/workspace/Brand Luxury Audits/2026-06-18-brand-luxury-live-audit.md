# Brand Luxury 1% Live Audit — YouTube — 2026-06-18

## Scope
- Focus surface: YouTube `youtube.com/@jeditrinupab`
- Rotation rationale: yesterday’s audit was Instagram, so today is YouTube.
- Live access: worked in public logged-out browser view. Audited channel homepage first impression, banner, avatar, description/link area, visible first video row, popular row, playlist row, Shorts row, and community posts preview.
- No public assets were edited, posted, scheduled, boosted, purchased, or changed.

## First-impression read
Jet’s YouTube already signals authority: 78.9K subscribers, 1.2K videos, a professional avatar, strong audience proof in the banner, and recent AI/Hermes/Claude/Codex content with healthy views. The main luxury gap is not expertise — it is premium visual control. The channel currently mixes high-status founder cues with creator-style urgency: a large red Subscribe graphic in the banner, multiple thumbnail color systems, loud yellow/red type, app logos, AI/robot/glow effects, and thumbnails with Jet’s face at different sizes and treatments. A more expensive-looking channel would keep the performance clarity, but add a consistent editorial frame: darker palette, fewer accents, calmer typography, and recurring Limitless Club metadata.

## Micro-upgrades

### 1) Banner detox: remove the giant red Subscribe button and turn the banner into an editorial masthead
- **Exact change:** For Jet approval, draft a refreshed banner using:
  - Background: `#17181A` / charcoal gradient.
  - Center headline: `คนไทย 1,000,000 คนต้องเก่ง AI` or shorter premium variant `AI Strategy for Thai Founders`.
  - Left/right proof photos kept, but desaturated or placed inside cleaner editorial frames.
  - Right side: Jet portrait + small Limitless Club lockup only.
  - Remove or heavily reduce the oversized red `SUBSCRIBE` button, bell icon, social icons, and cursor graphic.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Red subscribe graphics are native YouTuber-growth language, not premium founder-media language. Luxury brands do not ask loudly; they signal confidence, proof, and restraint.
- **Quick implementation note:** Keep the YouTube function of the banner, but let the actual YouTube Subscribe button do the CTA work below. Use the banner for brand memory and authority, not UI mimicry.
- **Priority:** High

### 2) Create a fixed thumbnail signature strip for every new long-form video
- **Exact change:** Add a consistent 6–8% height footer or top-left metadata strip:
  - `LIMITLESS CLUB`
  - category tag: `AI OS`, `AGENT PLAYBOOK`, `FOUNDER WORKFLOW`, `TOOL REVIEW`
  - tiny `@jeditrinupab`
  - optional bronze divider line `#94764A`
- **Why it increases luxury association:** The visible first row has strong ideas but inconsistent packaging: green coding visual, orange AI clash, blue Hermes medical icon, whiteboard business equation, and red warning marks. A fixed signature strip creates premium memory even when thumbnail concepts vary.
- **Quick implementation note:** Do not redesign old thumbnails yet. Apply the strip only to the next 5 uploads, then review click-through before retrofitting winners.
- **Priority:** High

### 3) Reduce thumbnail accent colors to one alert color per video
- **Exact change:** For future thumbnails, limit each cover to:
  - Base: black/graphite/white/warm ivory.
  - One accent: bronze for premium/editorial, or red only for true warning/conflict videos.
  - Avoid using yellow + red + blue + green in the same first-row visual field.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Premium design feels expensive because it looks controlled. The current homepage row reads high-energy but slightly busy: yellow Thai headline, red warning icon, blue tech UI, orange clash background, green code, and whiteboard marks all compete.
- **Quick implementation note:** Make a thumbnail QA rule: “If there are more than 2 accent colors, remove one before export.” Keep contrast high, but make the palette intentional.
- **Priority:** Medium

### 4) Standardize Jet’s face treatment: same crop logic, same lighting family, fewer cutout styles
- **Exact change:** Choose one YouTube face system:
  - Jet on right third or left third, never floating randomly.
  - Similar head size across thumbnails.
  - Soft contour shadow only, no heavy glow unless the concept requires it.
  - Expression range: focused / surprised / explaining — avoid over-cartooning.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Luxury channels build recognition through repetition. The visible thumbnails use Jet’s face effectively, but the face scale, edge treatment, and background intensity vary enough to feel more like creator packaging than a premium editorial system.
- **Quick implementation note:** Create a reusable `Jet Portrait Layer` set in the thumbnail template: 3 approved cutouts, 1 shadow style, 1 crop zone.
- **Priority:** Medium

### 5) Title packaging: make “Full Course” and tutorial labels feel more institutional
- **Exact change:** For course/tutorial videos, test a more premium naming format:
  - Current visible: `[Full Course] วิธีการสร้าง AI Operating System ด้วย ...`
  - Suggested: `AI Operating System: Full Course for Founders`
  - Thai option: `AI Operating System สำหรับเจ้าของธุรกิจ — Full Course`
  - Keep high-intent Thai keywords, but move brackets and utility labels into a cleaner editorial structure.
- **Why it increases luxury association:** Bracket-heavy title packaging feels like YouTube optimization. Editorial naming feels more like a serious media library or executive training catalog.
- **Quick implementation note:** Use only on new uploads first. Avoid changing high-performing historical titles until Jet approves an SEO-safe title cleanup.
- **Priority:** Medium

## Today’s recommended 1% upgrade
**Banner detox: remove the oversized red Subscribe/button graphics and turn the channel banner into a restrained Limitless Club editorial masthead.**

## Why this is the best 1% move
- It is the first premium association cue before viewers even judge individual videos.
- It preserves the strong mission/proof while removing the most “YouTuber” visual element.
- It is a one-asset improvement with high surface-area impact across every channel visit.
