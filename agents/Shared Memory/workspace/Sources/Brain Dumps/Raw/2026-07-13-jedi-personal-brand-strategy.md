## What To Write About
Stop overthinking. The brand builds itself as you post. Here's the menu — pick one and write:
- Extreme beliefs. "You don't need more people. You need better systems. Every new hire is a system failure until proven otherwise."
- Harsh truths. "Cheap clients are the most expensive. They pay the least, complain the most, stay the longest."
- Lessons from your own journey. The Izipizi story. The Glory Global betrayal. The ฿40M rebuild.
- "Damn, I wish I wrote that" ideas. Swipe a hook from Greg Isenberg or Hormozi and run it through your Thai-AI lens.
- Problems people face. "I have 50 employees and I'm drowning." → Here's the C.E.O. Loop.
- Arguments to content you read. React to an AI hype post with your real cost breakdown.
- Opinions on popular topics. "AI isn't making people dumb. People who stopped learning when they got comfortable were already on that path."
- Interesting facts. ฿700/mo for Claude Pro vs ฿25,000/mo for a junior employee. That math is not close.
- Your take on top-performing content. Remix your own winning videos. "AI Agent 0-1" (105K views) has more angles in it.
- Advice to your past self. "Stop building other people's brands. Go all-in on yours."
- Aspirational advice. Tell people what to do and why. "Replace one employee with one AI agent this month. Not to fire them — to see what's possible."
The through-line: just pick one and write the post. Your library of personal knowledge compounds. That "basic" post you think everyone knows? Half your audience has never heard it. The other half needs the reminder.
