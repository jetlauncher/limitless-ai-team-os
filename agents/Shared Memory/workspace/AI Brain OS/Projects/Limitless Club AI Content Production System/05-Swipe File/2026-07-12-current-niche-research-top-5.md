## What is working right now

### 1. Agentic workflow education with clear business outcome

Examples:

- Greg Isenberg — `Building AI Agents that actually work (Full Course)` — ~545K views, 2026-03-17.
- Nick Saraev — `AGENTIC WORKFLOWS: Build & Sell AI Automations (2026)` — ~288K views, 2025-12-21.
- Liam Ottley — `How to Build & Sell AI Agents in 2026` — ~211K views, 2026-03-22.

Pattern: longform full-course / complete guide / build-and-sell framing still works when it promises implementation, not vague AI hype.

### 2. Business-owner AI usage from known entrepreneurs

Examples:

- Alex Hormozi — `How to Win With AI in 2026` — ~780K views, 2026-03-31.

Pattern: business audience wants direct owner/operator translation: what to do with AI in a real company, not tool lists.

### 3. “What businesses actually want” / field-tested workflow proof

Example:

- Nate Herk — `I’ve Built 500 AI Workflows, This is What Businesses Want in 2026` — ~234K views, 2026-03-30.
- IG indexed result from same creator: “A Real Example of One of the Most Profitable Automations…”

Pattern: volume proof + market insight performs. “I built 500 workflows” is proof before teaching.

### 4. AI-first company / teamless operator framing

Examples:

- Y Combinator — `How to Build an AI-Native Services Company` — ~107K views, 2026-06-03.
- Silicon Valley Girl — `AI-First Playbook: Do a Team's Work With AI (2026) | Peter Yang` — ~52K views, 2026-06-16.
- X discussions: AI-native companies, agentic pods, one CEO + agents, agent swarms, MCP, internal company OS.

Pattern: people are interested in the org-design shift: fewer employees, more systems/agents, workflow mapping, company OS.

### 5. Contrarian “agents vs workflows / stop building agents wrong” short-form

Instagram search snippets show hooks like:

- “Stop building AI agents until you do this.”
- “Stop building AI agents every time a random task annoys you.”
- “AI agents automate decisions, not accountability.”
- “Everyone wants AI agents right now… workflow...”

Pattern: short-form winners are not generic “AI agents are amazing.” They are corrective/contrarian: owners are building the wrong thing, skipping workflow mapping, or automating without accountability.

## X / founder-operator current themes

Recent X/Grok search surfaced these repeated signals:

1. Pick painful workflows where errors cost money.
2. Manually validate with Claude/ChatGPT before coding.
3. Build smallest agent with logs, approvals, analytics.
4. Company OS in Markdown/GitHub/Notion + MCP + agents is becoming a serious operating pattern.
5. Uber-style “agentic pods”: pair AI builders with domain experts, shadow workflows, build custom internal agents.
6. Agentic content systems: automate research → formats → B-roll/captions → distribution.
7. Trust comes from permissions, logs, rollback, and human oversight — not pure autonomy.

## Top 5 Jedi-native content ideas

### 1. Before You Build an AI Agent, Map This Workflow First

Core angle: Most business owners should not start by “building an agent.” They should map one painful repeating workflow.

Why now: IG/X snippets show strong traction for “stop building agents wrong” and “agents vs workflows.”

Jedi edge: You can show Hermes/Limitless OS as a real workflow-first system.

### 2. I Replaced a 3-Person Marketing Team With One AI Workflow

Core angle: Case-study style proof. Show the team-role map: strategist, copywriter, coordinator → AI workflow + owner approval.

Why now: Nate Herk-style “what businesses actually want” and “profitable automation examples” are working.

Jedi edge: Thai business-owner version with actual student/business implementation.

### 3. The AI-First Company Playbook for Thai Business Owners

Core angle: Translate AI-native company / one CEO + agents / agentic pods into a practical Thai SME operating model.

Why now: AI-first company and AI-native services company content is trending in founder circles.

Jedi edge: You are not theory — you are operating a real multi-agent OS with Kelly/Blaze/Signal/Bolt/etc.

### 4. My 7-Day AI Agent Business Audit

Core angle: A simple challenge: in 7 days, identify one bottleneck, map workflow, build draft agent, run with human approval, measure result.

Why now: X playbooks emphasize manual validation, measurable workflows, logs/approvals before coding.

Jedi edge: Perfect for cohort lead magnet / workshop bridge.

### 5. AI Agents Automate Work, Not Accountability

Core angle: Contrarian trust/safety piece. Owners cannot abdicate taste, offer, brand, approval, or customer responsibility.

Why now: Current discussions emphasize guardrails, logs, permissions, rollback, human oversight.

Jedi edge: Strong premium/business-owner positioning: mature AI adoption, not toy automation.
